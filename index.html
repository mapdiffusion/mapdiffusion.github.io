Inferring Indoor Layouts from Walking Trajectories using Diffusion

In Submission

Demo Video

Abstract

This paper asks: is it possible to predict the indoor floorplan of a home from a few minutes of walking trajectories of a user inside her home? The problem is non-trivial because an infinite number of floorplans are candidate solutions for the given trajectories; how can one identify the correct floorplan? This paper leverages generative models to approach this question. The key idea is to train a diffusion model with floorplan-trajectory pairs, where the floorplans are drawn from public datasets, and the trajectories are synthetically generated via simulation. When designed correctly, the diffusion model learns a conditional distribution: given walking trajectories, the diffusion model can generate floorplan samples that have high likelihoods. To further filter the floorplans, we show that the user can be asked to walk a bit more in certain regions of the home, allowing the diffusion model to reduce the uncertainty in its predictions. We implement our model on the Stable Diffusion XL architecture, train on over 35,000 floorplans, and optimize for real-time operation. Results show promising floorplan accuracy, beating existing baselines and degrading gracefully when trajectories are too few. We believe our key idea of inverting trajectories to floorplans can be generalized to other inference applications.
