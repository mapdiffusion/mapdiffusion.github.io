Can Sparse Walking Patterns Disclose Spatial Maps?

In Submission

Demo Video

Abstract

Imagine users walking in an indoor environment and a sensor (e.g., their smartphone) measuring their walking trajectories. This paper asks: is it possible to infer the spatial map, or floorplan, from few minutes of the user's walking trajectories? The problem is non-trivial because an infinite number of floorplans can possibly explain the measured trajectories; which one is correct? This paper leverages generative models to approach this question. The key idea is to train a diffusion model with floorplan-trajectory pairs, where the floorplans are drawn from public datasets, and the trajectories are synthetically generated for these floorplans. When designed correctly, the diffusion model learns a conditional distribution p(x|y), where x denotes floorplans and y is the given trajectory measurements. Samples from this distribution give us floorplans that are highly likely solutions. To further narrow down the floorplans, we design a closed-loop method that requests the user to walk a bit more in a specific region, and uses the additional trajectory information to reduce the floorplan uncertainty. We implement our model on the Stable Diffusion XL architecture, train on over 35,000 floorplans, and optimize for real-time operation. Results show promising floorplan accuracy in both simulated and real-world settings and degrade gracefully when trajectories are sparse.
